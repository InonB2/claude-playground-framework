## Context for This Revision

BuildARPro is a stealth-stage startup. There is no waitlist, no beta NPS, no creator count, and no live users yet. The original Slide 9 (Traction/Demo) and associated one-pager "Traction" block contained placeholder text for metrics that do not exist. Pitching with unfilled placeholders is worse than pitching without traction — it signals you haven't done this before.

The fix: replace traction language with market-validated proof-of-thesis. Founders raise pre-seed on insight and opportunity, not on users. Stealth positioning done right signals strategic intent, not lack of progress.

## COLE'S QUALITY CHECK — Does This Hold Up Without Metrics?

**Does it sell the opportunity without traction?** Yes. The argument rests on:
1. Market size (verifiable, sourced)
2. Industrial proof-of-concept (AR assembly guidance is already a proven category)
3. Competitive gap (mapped and documented — the gap is real)
4. Execution credibility (stealth framing + specific milestones signals intent, not vaporware)

**What it cannot claim:**
- User validation ("users love it") — replaced with market-level validation ("the category is proven at industrial scale")
- NPS — dropped entirely
- Creator commitments — replaced with "creator acquisition is a funded milestone"

**What makes it stronger than a metrics-stuffed slide with fake numbers:**
Investors who've seen a hundred pitches know what "[insert NPS here]" means. A confident "we are in stealth" paired with sharp market evidence and a tight milestone plan reads as founder maturity, not inexperience. The Industrial AR proof point ($1.32B market growing at 21%) is the killer substitution — it answers the "does this work?" question without needing a single beta user.
