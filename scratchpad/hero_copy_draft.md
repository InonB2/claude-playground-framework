### Tagline (replaces "I build products that scale")

**Version A — Direct / Trump register:**

The PM who doesn't just plan — he ships.
```

```
Co-Founder. CPO. Builder.

**Recommendation: Version C** — it answers all three questions immediately:
- Who: Co-Founder, CPO, Builder (titles with texture)
- What: $2.5M raised, millions of users, six live products (proof, not claim)
- Why: "Not projected. Delivered." (differentiator — most PMs don't build)

### Body paragraph (replaces current subtitle)

**Current:**
> I build products that scale. Co-Founder & CPO of TouchE — raised $2.5M, shipped to millions of users, partnered with Paramount & Lion's Gate.

**Rewritten:**
```
I spent 6 years as Co-Founder & CPO of TouchE — an AI video platform I built from zero, raised $2.5M for, and shipped to millions of concurrent users across 4 countries. Paramount. Lion's Gate. JVP. That wasn't luck. That was judgment, speed, and the ability to lead teams and close deals at the same time.

Now I'm looking for the next problem worth owning.
```

**Why it's better:**
- Opens with a specific, credible claim ("6 years as Co-Founder & CPO")
- Named partners create authority without sounding like a resume
- "That wasn't luck. That was judgment, speed, and the ability to lead teams and close deals at the same time." — this is the HBR / Vinh Giang rhythm: short sentence after a long one, landing on the insight
- Final line ("Now I'm looking for the next problem worth owning") is a forward-facing hook — not passive job seeker energy
