**HOOK A — The counterintuitive take:**

Most PMs ship features. I've spent 10 years learning to kill them.

At TouchE, we built an AI interactive video platform. Raised $2.5M. Onboarded Paramount, JVP, Lion's Gate. The product I was proudest of? The one we *didn't* ship.

We had a "social viewing" feature — co-watch with friends, live reactions, shared playlists. The engineering team loved it. Early users said they wanted it.

We killed it 3 weeks before launch.

Here's why:

When I looked at the actual behavior data (not surveys — *behavior*), users who engaged with interactive overlays watched 34% longer. Users who watched "socially" left after 8 minutes. Every time.

The feature was solving a problem users *said* they had. Not the one they *actually* had.

The discipline to kill what you've already built — that's what separates product managers from feature factories.

What's the hardest product decision you've ever had to make?

---

**HOOK B — The founder signal:**

I built 3 products in 2024. Zero co-founders. Zero agency. Just me, a whiteboard, and enough conviction to ship.

TradePulse: a trading journal that tracks *emotions* alongside P&L (live in 3 weeks).
Family Flow: a Hebrew-first chore app for Israeli families (2 weeks, active users).
AiRakoon: enterprise AI product strategy for a GenAI startup (pilot closed).

None of them started with a business plan.

They started with a problem I couldn't stop thinking about.

That's my process: obsess over the problem until the solution becomes obvious. Then ship before you're ready.

What are you building?

---

**HOOK C — The PM job market take:**

I've been on both sides of the table.

As a hiring manager, I've interviewed 60+ PMs. Here's what I actually look for — none of it is on job descriptions:

1. **Can you tell me about a product decision that was wrong?** (Not the lesson. The *feeling* when you realized it.)
2. **What's a product you use daily that's broken in one specific way?** (If you can't answer instantly, you're not a PM, you're a manager.)
3. **What did you say no to last week?** (Roadmaps are mostly what you *don't* build.)

The best PMs I've ever hired couldn't recite their STAR stories perfectly. They were too busy thinking about the problem.

Senior PM interviews are broken. We're selecting for presentation skill when we should be selecting for product instinct.

What question do YOU wish interviewers asked?
